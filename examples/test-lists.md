# 47 Productivity Hacks That Actually Work

## Time Management

1. Time-block your calendar (90-min deep work blocks)
2. No meetings before 11 AM
3. Batch similar tasks together
4. Use the 2-minute rule (if it takes <2 min, do it now)
5. Set artificial deadlines (Parkinson's Law)
6. Track your time for 1 week (you'll be shocked)
7. Delete/delegate/defer (in that order)

## Focus

8. Close all browser tabs except one
9. Phone in another room during deep work
10. Noise-canceling headphones + lo-fi beats
11. Pomodoro: 25 min work, 5 min break
12. Say "no" by default, "yes" only if excited
13. Kill notifications (all of them)
14. Single-tasking > multitasking (always)

## Tools

15. TextExpander for common snippets
16. Alfred/Raycast for quick access
17. Obsidian for notes (plain Markdown)
18. Notion for project management
19. Superhuman for email speed
20. Linear for issue tracking

## Habits

21. Wake up at the same time every day
22. Morning routine (before checking email)
23. Exercise (even 10 min helps)
24. Read 30 pages/day
25. Journal 5 minutes (what went well?)
26. Sleep 7-8 hours (non-negotiable)
27. Weekly review (Sunday evening, 30 min)

## Energy

28. Caffeine only before noon
29. Protein-heavy breakfast
30. Walk after lunch (digestion + reset)
31. Stand desk (alternate sitting/standing)
32. Hydrate (1L before lunch)
33. No sugar crashes (avoid carb-heavy lunch)
34. Power nap (20 min, after lunch if needed)

## Communication

35. Email templates for common replies
36. Async-first (Loom videos > meetings)
37. Default to public channels (less DM noise)
38. Status updates on Friday (sync team)
39. "No meeting Wednesday" (deep work day)
40. End meetings 5 min early (buffer)

## Mental

41. Brain dump before bed (clear your mind)
42. Gratitude journal (3 things daily)
43. Meditation (10 min, any app)
44. Nature walks (disconnect)
45. Digital detox (1 day/month, no screens)
46. Celebrate small wins (dopamine boost)
47. Ship > perfect (done is better than perfect)

---

**The meta-hack:** Don't try all 47 at once. Pick 3, master them, then add more. Consistency > intensity.
